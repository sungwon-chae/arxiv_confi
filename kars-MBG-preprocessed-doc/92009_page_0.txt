Mercedes-Benz Korea partners up with SOCAR for bringing the largest supply of electric vehicles to the car-sharing industry- Mercedes-Benz Korea and SOCAR signed a car-sharing service agreement to supply 200 units of the full electric vehicle, the new EQC, by stages- This agreement marks the largest supply of electric vehicles in the car-sharing service industry, and paves the way for a broader base of eco-friendly mobility in Korea- Featuring unique design, dynamic driving performance and excellent efficiency, the new EQC will be available for service starting from early June in Seoul and Gyeonggi and followed by supply in Jeju region in July (Seoul, June 1, 2020) ? Mercedes-Benz Korea Ltd.(President & CEO Dimitris Psillakis) announced on June 1 that it concluded an agreement with SOCAR to supply 200 units of the new EQC, the first full electric vehicle from Mercedes-Benz?s EQ brand, to SOCAR?s car-sharing service.Marking the largest-ever electric vehicle supply volume in the Korean car-sharing market, this agreement will give momentum to the efforts of the both companies to bring more eco-friendly vehicles onto the streets of Korea. 200 units of the new EQC in total will be delivered in stages by July with 80 units of those being available for service in Seoul and Gyeonggi first starting early June, followed by supply in Jeju region in July. SOCAR customers will be able to experience the new EQC conveniently in around 150 SOCAR zones( in Seoul, Gyeonggi and Jeju with the SOCAR application.The new EQC is the first-ever full electric vehicle launched by Mercedes-Benz?s product and technology brand EQ in October 2019. Featuring a distinctive design along with dynamic driving performance and outstanding efficiency thanks to a new electric drivetrain, the new EQC received two awards for the ?Car of the Year 2020? by the Korea Automobile Journalists Association and the JoongAng Ilbo in the respective categories of the ?Green Car of the Year? and the ?Future Mobility of the Year?.Equipped with an electric motor at the front and rear axles, the new EQC generates a maximum output of 408hp and maximum torque of 78.0kg.m, propelling from a standstill to 100km/h in a mere 5.1 seconds for an explosive driving performance. The new EQC is available to drive more than 309 km on a single charge, and its battery can be charged to 80% in around 40 minutes(( with 110kW quick charging. Further, the new EQC is equipped with the latest driving assistance technologies and the MBUX infotainment system that includes exclusive functions for EVs, offering excellence in safety and convenience.?It is a great pleasure for us to partner up with Korea?s number one car-sharing service provider SOCAR to provide Korean customers with an opportunity to experience the new EQC,? said Lee Sang-kuk, Vice President Sales at Mercedes-Benz Korea, who attended the ceremony. ?We hope customers will be able to take a glimpse in the future of mobility along with the distinctive strengths of the new EQC, and we will continue to do our utmost to broaden the base of eco-friendly mobility in Korea.??Through this partnership, we will be able to expand our experience in electric vehicles as well as the world's top premium automobile brand,? said Wi Hyun-jong Wi, Vice President at SOCAR. ?As car-sharing services are becoming more mainstream and diverse, we will actively cooperate with manufacturers to respond quickly to customer demands.?Meanwhile, Mercedes-Benz Korea operates a total of 59 showrooms and 70 service centers across Korea, among which five showrooms and seven service centers have around 50 EQ specialists including sales and product experts, technicians and service advisers, who are well-versed in the products and technologies of the EQ brand.
